I'm Dan Kibet, a full stack developer based between Eldoret and Nairobi, building software that Kenyan businesses run on every day.

01 WHO

I build production software end to end, covering database schema, API, interface, and deployment. Not a slice of the stack handed off to someone else: the whole thing, owned from first commit to live system.

My through line is simple. I like problems where software replaces something slow and manual, such as a paper ledger, a phone based credit book, or a rigid bus contract, with a system people actually open every morning. That's the work I find worth doing.

02 PATH

I trained at Moringa School, where my capstone (MiniTruck, an "Uber for the school run") pushed me to ship a deployed, working marketplace rather than a demo. That set the standard for everything since: build the real thing.

From there I moved into independent client work. A multi module POS and finance platform for an agribusiness. A dairy farm management system with lactation modeling. Each one a real business, a real deployment, real users depending on it.

03 NOW

I'm a freelance developer working with clients across Kenya. Two systems are live and in daily use: Grade Agrovet, a complete POS and finance platform built for an established agribusiness, and Smart Dairy, a farm management system with lactation curve modeling and estrus detection. Both replaced manual processes that were slowing real businesses down.

04 BEYOND

Away from the editor, I think seriously about how systems work at a larger scale: governance, political economy, institutional design. How do you build things that hold up, that serve people, that don't quietly rot?

It's the same instinct that shows up in my code: get the underlying model right and the rest follows. I care about Kenya at a systems level, and I want to build the kind of software that earns a place in how the country actually runs.
